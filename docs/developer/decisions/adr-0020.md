# ADR-0020 — Device catalog: LoRaWAN-native actuators only; proprietary systems as the buy-side benchmark

- Status: Proposed
- Date: 2026-06-12

## Context

The economic viability study (`docs/developer/analysis/`) compares a self-built
LoRaWAN heating-control network (**Make**) against complete commercial systems
(**Buy**). Preliminary research established two facts
([preliminary-research.md](../analysis/preliminary-research.md)):

1. LoRaWAN-native radiator thermostats exist off-the-shelf as Class A products
   (MClimate Vicki, dnt LW-eTRV, Eurotronic Stella R/Pro, Milesight WT101,
   Micropelt/DEOS energy-harvesting), ~€67–97/valve retail.
2. The consumer smart-TRV market is owned by **non-LoRaWAN** protocols —
   Homematic IP (proprietary BidCos 868 MHz), Zigbee, DECT-ULE — and there is
   **no commercial Homematic-to-LoRaWAN actuator bridge**; the only open bridge
   (LoRaBridge) forwards Zigbee *sensors* uplink-only, with no setpoint downlink.

## Decision

The Make-side device catalog contains **only LoRaWAN-native actuators**.
Proprietary smart-heating systems (Homematic IP, tado°, Bosch, Danfoss, …) never
enter the catalog; they appear **exclusively on the Buy side** as the make-vs-buy
benchmark. In the glossary, an **Actuator** is by definition a LoRaWAN-native TRV.
Qualification is by **protocol** (does it speak a LoRaWAN join), not by brand
name.

## Rationale

- The PO's stated ambition is **pure LoRaWAN from open-market components**. Mixing
  a proprietary RF island into the Make side breaks the architecture that gives
  Make its value: one open LNS, one API, one data pipeline, no per-vendor hub.
- **Bridging is not viable** for actuation — no Homematic→LoRaWAN setpoint path
  exists, and building a custom bridge re-creates lock-in for large effort.
- Excluding proprietary devices from Make does not lose their value — it surfaces
  it, fairly, as the **Buy** comparison.

Alternatives rejected: (a) allow a cheaper proprietary valve on the Make side —
it is not LoRaWAN and defeats the open-network thesis; (b) build a custom
LoRaWAN↔Homematic bridge — large effort, no open standard, re-introduces lock-in.

## Consequences

- Make hardware cost uses LoRaWAN TRV prices (~€67–97/valve), higher per unit than
  consumer Zigbee/Homematic valves. This is an honest input and is precisely what
  the make-vs-buy comparison weighs.
- The MClimate Vicki's documented ChirpStack v4 downlink codec makes it the
  reference actuator for any later integration on whz-lora.
- "Homematic device" is explicitly **not** an Actuator. A testbed device that
  emits a LoRaWAN join qualifies regardless of brand; the live `Invalid MIC`
  device is an operational matter, separate from the study catalog.
